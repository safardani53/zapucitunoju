Claire Kittle | Early Years 

Claire Kittle was born on 24th May 1994 in Dubuque, Iowa, USA. She is the oldest child of Marty Till and Shelly Till. 

She also has a younger brother named Riley Till. During their childhood, they spent countless hours playing one-on-one games.

Likewise, both of them were former Wahlert standout players. Both of them dreamt of playing for the University of Iowa.

Being four years older, Claire became a perfect role model for her brother. Later, they became the first brother and sister to play collegiate basketball for Iowa. 

Moreover, sports run in the family blood. Claire’s mother, Selley, clinched the 1987 Iowa State basketball championship. Later, she played for Drake University.

Similarly, her dad attended the University of Northern Iowa and played collegiate baseball. 

He also played semi-pro ball for Farley Hawks. Later, in 2014 he earned a place in the Dubuque County Baseball Hall of Fame. 

Furthermore, Shelly coached Claire, and Marty coached Riley in their early years.

Claire Kittle | Education

High School

Claire Kittle attended Wahlert Catholic High School. During her senior year, she was nominated for Mc Donalds All-American. But she could not make the final cut.

However, Claire has a two-time All-State, three-time IBCA Select Team, and four-time first-team All-Conference selection. 

Likewise, Claire led Wahlert to a conference title and appearance in the state tournament in her freshman season. That season she averaged 12.4 PPG.

Next season, she played only 17 games due to injury. But averaged better at 15.4 PPG and 6 RPG. 

During her junior season, she averaged 12.7 PPG and 6 RPGs. Later, her senior season was limited to 10 games due to injury. 

She finished her final year recording 14.4 PPG, 8RP, 2.5 APG, and 2 BPG. ESPN ranked her as a 3-star recruit. 

In addition, Claire ranks third in all-time leading scorer Dubuque Wahlert’s history scoring 1,011 points.

Besides basketball, she was also a standout track athlete. Moreover, she has two All-State selections to her name. 

She placed second in the high jump during her junior season, tying for the record. Later, in her junior year, she placed third and helped her team to a state title. 

Furthermore, Claire was good academically. She graduated from high school as a high honor roll student. 

College

In 2012, Claire enrolled at the University of Iowa under the basketball scholarship program.

However, her freshman season was again obstructed by injury, and she played 16 games. 

Later, in her sophomore season, she appeared in all 36 games. Coming off the bench, Claire recorded 2.7 PPG and 2.9 RPG. 

Likewise, she appeared in 29 games in her junior season, shooting 56.6 from the floor. 

During her senior season, she played 14 games. But Claire suffered from an achronic knee injury, prematurely ending her basketball career.

Later, on 22nd February 2016, she underwent surgery. Claire graduated in 2016 with a health and human physiology degree. 

Besides basketball, she also served as Health and Wellness student ambassador with the Fuel Up to Play 60 program.  

The program aimed to help youths reach their fitness goals through step-by-step guidance.
